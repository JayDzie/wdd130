Learn More About Our Rivers.

The Colorado River flows through seven U.S. states: Wyoming, Colorado, New Mexico, Utah, Nevada, Arizona, and California.

Colorado River.

The Colorado River, a lifeline of the American Southwest, winds through seven U.S. states, offering a thrilling adventure for water enthusiasts. Known for the iconic Grand Canyon, the Colorado River provides breathtaking scenery, challenging rapids, and opportunities for unforgettable rafting expeditions.

The Arkansas River flows through Colorado. Notable Feature: Rafters on the Arkansas River enjoy the backdrop of the Rocky Mountains, including sections like the Royal Gorge and Bighorn Sheep Canyon.

Snake River.

The Snake River, originating in Wyoming and flowing through the majestic landscapes of Idaho and Oregon, is a paradise for whitewater rafting. Renowned for its picturesque canyons and diverse wildlife, the Snake River offers a mix of exhilarating rapids and tranquil stretches, catering to both seasoned rafters and beginners.

Location: The New River flows through North Carolina, Virginia, and West Virginia. Notable Feature: Known as the "Grand Canyon of the East," the New River offers a scenic journey through the Appalachian Mountains.

Arkansas River.

The Arkansas River, meandering through Colorado, is a premier destination for rafting enthusiasts. Surrounded by the towering peaks of the Rocky Mountains, the river boasts a variety of rapids suitable for all skill levels. From the challenging Royal Gorge to the family-friendly Bighorn Sheep Canyon, the Arkansas River provides a dynamic rafting experience amid breathtaking mountain scenery.

Location: The Chattooga River forms the border between Georgia and South Carolina. Notable Feature: Designated as a Wild and Scenic River, the Chattooga is known for its pristine and untouched natural surroundings.

New River

The New River, flowing through the Appalachian Mountains in West Virginia, is recognized for its diverse rapids and rich history. Offering a blend of both calm waters and thrilling rapids, this river provides an exciting journey through picturesque gorges and dense forests. Rafters on the New River can expect a perfect balance of adventure and natural beauty.

Location: The Kenai River is located in the state of Alaska. Notable Feature: Rafting on the Kenai River provides a unique opportunity to experience Alaska's wilderness, including encounters with salmon and other wildlife.

Chattooga River

The Chattooga River, cascading through the border of Georgia and South Carolina, is a designated Wild and Scenic River known for its pristine wilderness and challenging rapids. As the only river in the Southeast with a "Wild" designation, the Chattooga promises an unspoiled and thrilling rafting experience, surrounded by lush forests and striking waterfalls.

Originating in Wyoming, the Snake River flows through Idaho and Oregon. The Snake River provides stunning views of the Teton Range and Snake River Canyon.

Kenai River

The Kenai River, located in Alaska, is a unique destination for rafting, offering a journey through pristine wilderness and stunning landscapes. Rafting on the Kenai provides the opportunity to witness diverse wildlife, including salmon and bald eagles, while navigating through glacial-fed waters. This northern gem offers a serene yet exhilarating rafting experience in the heart of Alaska's natural wonders.
